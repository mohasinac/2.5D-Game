# Generation 4 — Parts Glossary (Beyblade X)

**Sources:** beyblade.fandom.com (cached 2026-05-19); individual Bit/Blade docs in `docs/learning/concepts/`
**Covers:** BX Basic Line (BX-##) · Unique Line (UX-##) · Custom Line (CX-##)
**System:** Blade + Ratchet + Bit (3 parts standard; Custom Line adds Lock Chip + Assist Blade = 5 parts)

> **Measurement key:** Values prefixed `~` are estimates from bey doc part tables. Values noted as "confirmed" appear in product documentation or verified fan measurements. Custom Line part weights account for Main Blade + Assist Blade separately.

## BITS (Performance Tips)

The Bit is the bottom component — equivalent to gen1 Blade Base tip and gen2/gen3 Driver. It governs movement pattern, Xtreme Dash eligibility, and spin retention. Bits use a universal bayonet socket fitting and are fully interchangeable across all BX Blades and Ratchets.

**Xtreme Dash key** — `✅` = rubber or gear tip contacts Xtreme Line rail directly; `⚡` = gear-assisted rail contact; `❌` = plastic/sharp tip cannot grip rail.

|------|-----------|----------|---------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| F | Flat | rubber_flat | ~5 g | ✅ primary | Aggressive wide orbit | Low | Flagship attack Bit; fastest spin decay; most reliable Xtreme Dash activation. Most common tournament attack Bit. Used by DranSword 3-60F. |
| LF | Low Flat | rubber_flat | ~5 g | ✅ primary | Aggressive wide orbit, lower profile | Low | F variant with ~1 mm shorter body; lower CoG keeps rail contact angle flatter — fewer deflections on Xtreme Line entry. Marginally more consistent Dash than F. Used by SharkEdge 3-60LF. |
| GF | Gear Flat | rubber_flat | ~6 g | ⚡ gear-assisted | Wide orbit, gear self-propelled | Low–Med | Rubber flat tip with outer gear ring mechanism. Gear ring grips rail and spins independently, boosting Dash force beyond standard F. Heavier than F due to gear housing. Used by PhoenixWing 9-60GF. |
| R | Rush | rubber_flat | ~5 g | ✅ primary | Aggressive wide orbit, high burst rate | Low | Rubber flat similar to F; named variant emphasizing high-speed rushing attack. Slightly different rubber compound from F — used in Unique Line products. Used by DranDagger 4-60R. |

## RATCHETS

The Ratchet is the middle structural component. It sets burst resistance (via tooth count) and bey height (via height unit). Named in `[teeth]-[height]` format.

**Standard heights:** 60 (low, stable), 70 (medium), 80 (tall, aggressive contact angle).
**Tooth range:** 0 (zero teeth — cannot Burst) through 9 (maximum Burst resistance).

```
Burst resistance scale:

  0 teeth ——————————————————————————————— 9 teeth
  Cannot     Low    Moderate    High      Max
  Burst     resist   resist    resist    resist
  (GhostCircle  (DranSword   (ValkyrieVolt  (PhoenixWing
   0-80GB)        3-60F)        4-70V)         9-60GF)
```

## ASSIST BLADES (Custom Line — CX products only)

The Custom Line (CX-##) adds two extra components beyond the standard 3-part stack: a **Main Blade** (the primary attack surface) and an **Assist Blade** (a secondary blade ring that augments contact geometry). The standard Gear Chip is also replaced by a **Lock Chip** (see next section). Full CX assembly: Lock Chip + Main Blade + Assist Blade + Ratchet + Bit.

Assist Blades sit below the Main Blade and above the Ratchet. They add additional contact wings or defensive flanges to the overall assembly, increasing total blade surface area and — in attack types — delivering secondary smash contacts during the same rotation cycle as the Main Blade's primary hit.

```
Standard BX stack:          Custom Line (CX) stack:

  [Gear Chip]                 [Lock Chip]
  [   Blade   ]  ─────>       [  Main Blade  ]
  [  Ratchet  ]               [ Assist Blade ]
  [    Bit    ]               [   Ratchet    ]
                              [     Bit      ]
```

### Known Assist Blades

| Name | Notation | ~Weight | Bey | Contact Zone(s) | Function |
|------|----------|---------|-----|----------------|----------|
| Slash | S (in S4-70V) | ~7 g | ValkyrieVolt S4-70V | smash, upper_attack | Supplementary blade ring that augments Volt Main Blade's three-bladed attack geometry; adds secondary contact wings at intermediate angles, increasing the number of smash contacts per rotation cycle. Shape references the slash/saber motif of the Valkyrie lineage. |

## LOCK CHIPS (Custom Line — CX products only)

Standard BX beys use a **Gear Chip** (decorative spirit emblem snapped into the Blade center slot — not structurally functional). Custom Line replaces this with a **Lock Chip**: a larger, separately-mounted clip that provides structural reinforcement to the Main Blade assembly. The Lock Chip can be swapped independently — unlike the embedded Gear Chip.

| Name | ~Weight | Bey | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| Valkyrie Metal Lock Chip | ~8 g | ValkyrieVolt S4-70V | Plastic base + metal top layer; ~4 g heavier than a standard Lock Chip (~4 g); raised center hole avoids launcher protrusion interference. **Tournament restriction: maximum 1 Valkyrie Metal Lock Chip per deck** (B4 and WBO ruling). Both Valkyrie AND Emperor Metal Lock Chips may each be used simultaneously in one deck if using separate beys. |

## GEAR CHIPS (Standard BX/UX — decorative spirit emblems)

Gear Chips are thematic spirit motif chips that snap into the center slot of standard Blades. They are decorative and structurally non-functional in standard play — weight is negligible (~1–2 g). Their names identify the spirit/character lineage of the Blade.
